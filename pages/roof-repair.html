Common Roof Repairs

Utah's climate puts roofs through extreme conditions — heavy snow, freeze-thaw cycles, intense summer UV, and seasonal hailstorms. Over time, these forces create wear that needs professional attention before small problems turn into expensive ones.

The most common roof repairs we handle across the Wasatch Front include:

  • Leak Repair: Tracking down and sealing active leaks, whether they are coming through damaged shingles, failed flashing, or deteriorated sealant around penetrations
  • Missing or Damaged Shingles: Replacing shingles that have been blown off by wind, cracked by hail, or worn out by age and UV exposure
  • Flashing Repair: Resealing or replacing the metal flashing around chimneys, skylights, walls, and roof transitions where most leaks originate
  • Pipe Boot Replacement: The rubber boots around plumbing vent pipes dry out and crack after 10 to 15 years, creating a common and preventable leak point
  • Valley Repair: Roof valleys channel large volumes of water and are prone to wear, rust, and debris buildup that causes leaks
  • Gutter & Fascia Repair: Damaged or detached gutters and rotted fascia boards that allow water to penetrate behind the roofline

Our Repair Process

Every roof repair starts with an honest assessment. We inspect the problem area and the surrounding roof to make sure we are fixing the root cause — not just patching a symptom that will come back in six months.

  • Inspection: We get on the roof, identify the source of the problem, and check for any related damage that is not immediately obvious
  • Honest Recommendation: We tell you whether a repair will solve the problem or whether the damage is extensive enough to warrant replacement. We never upsell
  • Matching Materials: We source shingles and materials that match your existing roof as closely as possible for a seamless appearance
  • Professional Repair: Our crews complete the repair using manufacturer-recommended techniques and materials
  • Post-Repair Verification: We verify the repair with water testing when appropriate and provide photos of the completed work

Repair vs. Replacement

Not every roof problem requires a full replacement, and not every repair is worth the investment. Knowing the difference saves you money and headaches. Here is how to think about it:

Repair makes sense when:

  • The damage is isolated to one area or a small number of shingles
  • Your roof is less than 15 years old and in otherwise good condition
  • The leak is caused by a specific failure point like a pipe boot or flashing
  • The cost of repair is significantly less than your insurance deductible

Replacement makes sense when:

  • Your roof is 20 to 25 years old with widespread wear
  • Storm damage covers multiple slopes or large areas
  • You are dealing with recurring leaks in different locations
  • Your insurance has approved a full replacement claim
