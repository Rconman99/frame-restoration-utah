How to File a Storm Damage Roof Insurance Claim in Utah

1Document the damage immediately

Take photos and video of all visible roof damage from the ground. Include close-ups of damaged shingles, dented gutters, broken flashing, and any interior water stains. Note the date and time of the storm.

2Contact your insurance company

Call your homeowners insurance provider within 48 hours to report the damage and open a claim. Write down your claim number, adjuster name, and timeline. Most Utah policies require claims within one year.

3Schedule a professional roof inspection

Contact a licensed Utah roofing contractor for a free storm damage inspection. A professional identifies damage invisible from the ground. Verify the contractor holds a Utah DOPL license at dopl.utah.gov.

4Meet with the insurance adjuster on-site

Have your roofing contractor present when the adjuster inspects. They can point out damage the adjuster might miss and ensure the scope covers all necessary repairs.

5Review the estimate and approve repairs

Compare the insurance estimate with your contractor's scope. If the estimate falls short, your contractor can file a supplement with additional documentation.

6Complete repairs and final walkthrough

After installation, do a final walkthrough to verify all work meets specifications. Keep your certificate of completion and all documentation for your records.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does homeowners insurance cover storm damage to my roof?

Yes, most Utah homeowners insurance policies cover sudden storm damage including hail, wind, and fallen trees. Coverage typically applies minus your deductible. Age-related wear is not covered. Frame Roofing Utah provides free storm damage inspections and handles all insurance documentation and adjuster coordination at no additional cost.

How do I know if my roof has hail damage?

Hail damage signs include dented or bruised shingles, granule loss exposing the asphalt mat, cracked or missing shingles, dented gutters or downspouts, and damaged roof vents or flashing. According to NOAA, Utah's Wasatch Front averages 3-5 significant hailstorms per year between May and September. A professional inspection is the most reliable way to assess damage.

How long do I have to file a storm damage insurance claim in Utah?

Most Utah insurance policies require claims to be filed within one year of the storm event, though some policies have shorter windows. We recommend filing as soon as possible after discovering damage. Frame Roofing Utah documents all damage with photos and measurements to support your claim and meets with your adjuster on-site.

What is the difference between a storm chaser and a local roofing contractor?

Storm chasers are out-of-state contractors who travel to areas after major storms, often using high-pressure sales tactics and providing limited warranty support. Local contractors like Frame Roofing Utah are licensed under Utah DOPL, carry local insurance, have a physical business address, and stand behind their work long-term. Always verify a contractor's Utah license at dopl.utah.gov.

How quickly can you respond to storm damage in Utah?

Frame Roofing Utah typically responds to storm damage calls within 24-48 hours across our 40+ service areas along the Wasatch Front. For active leaks or structural damage, we offer emergency tarping services to prevent further interior damage while the full repair or replacement is coordinated.
