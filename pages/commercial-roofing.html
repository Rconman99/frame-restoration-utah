How to Create a Commercial Roof Maintenance Plan in Utah

1Schedule a baseline roof inspection

Hire a licensed commercial roofing contractor to inspect your entire roof system — membrane condition, seam integrity, flashings, drainage, penetrations, and equipment curbs.

2Identify and prioritize repairs

Categorize issues by severity: critical (active leaks), moderate (deteriorating seams, minor ponding), and preventive (sealant renewal, drain cleaning). Address critical items immediately.

3Establish a biannual inspection schedule

Schedule inspections in spring (after freeze-thaw) and fall (before snow). Utah's UV, hail, and temperature swings make biannual inspections essential. Add post-storm inspections.

4Set up preventive maintenance tasks

Clear drains quarterly, re-seal flashings annually, check HVAC curbs, remove debris, and verify roof hatches. Preventive maintenance extends lifespan by 5-10 years.

5Document everything for warranty compliance

Maintain a log with dated photos, receipts, and reports. Most warranties require documented maintenance. This documentation is also critical for insurance claims.

6Budget for long-term roof lifecycle costs

Budget 1-2% of replacement cost annually for maintenance. A well-maintained commercial roof can exceed expected lifespan by 25-30%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of commercial roofing systems do you install?

Frame Roofing Utah installs all major commercial roofing systems including TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin), EPDM (rubber membrane), modified bitumen, built-up roofing (BUR), metal panel systems, and standing seam metal. The best system depends on your building's structure, budget, and energy efficiency requirements.

How long does a commercial roof last in Utah?

Commercial roof lifespan in Utah varies by system: TPO membranes typically last 20-30 years, EPDM rubber roofs 25-30 years, metal roofing systems 40-60 years, and built-up roofing 20-25 years. Utah's UV exposure at elevation, freeze-thaw cycles, and occasional hail can affect lifespan. Regular maintenance extends the life of any system.

Do you handle commercial roof maintenance programs?

Yes, Frame Roofing Utah offers scheduled commercial roof maintenance programs including biannual inspections, drainage clearing, membrane integrity checks, flashing inspection, and minor repair services. Preventive maintenance can extend your commercial roof's lifespan by 5-10 years and helps maintain manufacturer warranty compliance.

Can you work on occupied commercial buildings?

Yes, Frame Roofing Utah regularly performs roofing work on occupied commercial buildings with minimal disruption. We coordinate scheduling around your business operations and can perform work in phases if needed. Safety protocols include perimeter protection, dust control, and clear communication throughout the project.

Does insurance cover commercial storm damage?

Most commercial property insurance policies in Utah cover storm damage from hail, wind, and other sudden weather events. Frame Roofing Utah provides comprehensive damage documentation with photos, measurements, and detailed reports for your insurance adjuster. We handle the entire claims process to maximize your coverage.
