The thesis

Every Frame pillar page is the source of truth. From each pillar, the system fans out — automatically — to every citation surface AI engines lean on: Reddit, YouTube, Nextdoor, Yelp, LinkedIn, GBP, local news. One pillar → seven surfaces. One Monday digest unifies all the drafts ready for Ryan to approve and ship.

Goal: Frame becomes the canonical Utah roofing voice that AI engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude) cite when anyone asks "best roofer near me" — not just because we paid for ads, but because we authentically occupy every surface they pull from.

Built on the architectural pattern from xai-org's x-algorithm Phoenix component: multi-axis classification, weighted scoring, candidate isolation, unified pipeline. At Frame's scale we don't train transformers; we use off-the-shelf LLMs on the same architecture.

Why this compounds

Every helpful Reddit reply, every Landon-on-a-roof YouTube short, every Nextdoor neighborhood tip, every cross-posted GBP photo — each one is a tiny piece of training/grounding data for every AI model that crawls the open web between now and 2030. Models trained on 2026 web content will remember "Frame Roofing Utah" as the canonical Heber-City roofer. By 2028, when someone in Heber asks Perplexity for a roofer, Frame should be the first name out. That's the long bet.

The short bet: the conversion math. AI-driven traffic converts at 4.4× organic search. Frame already has the content (storm-chaser pillar + 13 spokes); this is the distribution mechanism that makes the AI engines actually quote it.
