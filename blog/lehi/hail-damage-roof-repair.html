How to Spot Hail Damage on Your Lehi Roof

Hail damage isn't always obvious at first glance. Some damage is visible from the ground, while other damage requires a professional inspection. Here's what to look for:

Signs of Hail Damage on Asphalt Shingles

Asphalt shingles show hail damage as rounded dents or bruises. When hail hits, it often knocks away the protective granule coating, exposing the darker asphalt beneath. Look for:

  • Round indentations – the most obvious sign; these dents look like someone tapped the shingle with a hammer
  • Missing or knocked-off granules – dark spots appearing where shingles were hit; over time, missing granules accelerate shingle deterioration
  • Cracked or split shingles – severe hail can crack shingles, creating entry points for water
  • Clustered damage patterns – hail doesn't hit randomly; you'll see multiple damaged shingles in the same area, often in linear or circular patterns

Signs on Metal Roofing

If your Lehi home has metal roofing, hail damage is even more visible—the impact creates permanent dents that can't be repaired without replacement.

Hidden Damage to Look For

Not all hail damage is visible from the ground. Damage can also occur on:

  • Gutters and downspouts – dented metal shows the hail's force
  • Skylights and vent covers – cracked glass or broken covers
  • Air conditioning units – dents in metal casings
  • The roof itself – damage hidden under shingles that won't be visible until a professional inspection

Types of Hail Damage and Material-Specific Effects

Different roofing materials respond to hail impact differently. Understanding how hail affects your specific roof helps you recognize damage and assess severity.

Asphalt Shingle Damage

Asphalt shingles are among the most common roofing materials in Lehi, and hail can cause significant damage. Small hail (pea to marble-sized) may cause granule loss without structural damage. Larger hail (golf-ball-sized or bigger) causes dents, cracks, and shingle splitting. Even small dents accelerate the aging process because granule loss exposes the asphalt to UV damage.

Wood Shake Damage

Wood shakes in some Lehi homes are vulnerable to hail splitting. Hail can cause cracks that run along the grain, creating paths for water infiltration. These cracks may not be immediately obvious but can lead to rot and structural damage over time.

Metal Roof Damage

Metal roofing shows hail damage as permanent dents. While metal doesn't crack or split like shingles, dents can collect water and create weak spots. Large dents on standing seam roofs can compromise the seams themselves.

Tile and Slate Damage

These premium materials are brittle and can crack or shatter under hail impact. Cracked tiles must be replaced to prevent water damage.
