Why Insurance Claims Matter After Storm Damage

When a severe storm hits Draper, the damage happens fast. High winds, hail, or fallen trees can compromise your roof in minutes, leaving your home vulnerable to water infiltration and further structural damage. The difference between filing a successful insurance claim and paying upfront can be thousands of dollars.

Most homeowners in the 84020 area don't realize they have coverage for storm damage. Insurance companies expect you to document damage quickly and hire qualified contractors to help with the process. Frame Restoration has helped dozens of Draper homeowners navigate claims successfully, and we know exactly what adjusters are looking for.

Acting within the first 48 hours of a storm is critical. The longer you wait, the more water damage spreads, and adjusters may assume the damage was pre-existing. Let's walk through what you need to know about roof insurance claims in Draper.

Common Storm Damage Types in Draper

Draper's elevation and location in the Wasatch Front mean spring and early summer storms can be severe. We commonly see three types of storm damage that qualify for insurance claims:

  • Hail damage: Hail dents shingles, creating weak spots where water pools and eventually leaks. Even small hail can significantly shorten roof lifespan.
  • Wind damage: Strong winds tear shingles away, expose underlayment, and can damage flashing around chimneys and vents. Wind-driven damage is the most common claim we handle.
  • Tree damage: Falling branches or trees directly puncture roofs or cause widespread impact damage. This often qualifies for the highest claim payouts because it's sudden and visible.

In Draper neighborhoods like Promontory and South Draper, we've handled numerous claims for wind and hail damage after spring storms. Insurance companies understand these risks and will typically cover repairs if you document the damage properly.

When Should I File an Insurance Claim?

File immediately after the storm passes and you've confirmed it's safe to be outside. Document damage with photos or video while fresh. Contact your insurance agent within 24 hours, and get a professional inspection from Frame Roofing Utah within 48 hours. In Draper 84020, delays can mean additional water damage that adjusters may not cover if it's considered negligence.

Step-by-Step Insurance Claims Process

Understanding the claims process reduces stress and helps you avoid costly mistakes. Here's how it works for roof damage in Draper:

1. Document Everything Immediately

Before the adjuster arrives, take clear photos and video of all visible damage. Shoot from multiple angles in good lighting. Document damage to gutters, flashing, vents, and the ground around your home as well. Write down the date and time of the storm. These photos are your evidence if disputes arise later.

2. Call Your Insurance Company

Report the claim within 24 hours. Provide your policy number, the date of the storm, and a brief description of the damage. Your insurer will assign an adjuster and schedule an inspection. Ask when the adjuster will visit so you can arrange for Frame Roofing Utah to be present—our team can answer technical questions and ensure nothing gets missed.

3. Get a Professional Roof Inspection

Frame Roofing Utah's inspectors are trained to identify both obvious and hidden damage. We document everything with photos, measurements, and detailed notes. Having a professional inspection protects you because adjusters respect contractor evaluations. We're familiar with how insurers in the Draper area work and what documentation they need to approve claims quickly.

4. The Insurance Adjuster Inspection

The adjuster will visit your home, climb onto the roof (or use binoculars for safety), and assess damage. They'll note the roof's age, condition, and type of material. Be prepared to ask questions about their assessment. If you disagree with their findings, you can request a second opinion or have your own inspector dispute specific findings.

5. Review the Estimate

Your insurer sends a written estimate for repairs. This is often called a "property damage estimate" and includes materials, labor, and sometimes additional costs. Review it carefully. If the estimate seems low or omits damage you saw, request a revision. Frame Roofing Utah can help you review the estimate and identify any discrepancies.

6. Choose Your Contractor and Schedule Repairs

What Adjusters Look For During Inspections

Insurance adjusters are trained to distinguish between storm damage and wear-and-tear or negligence. Knowing what they're assessing helps you prepare:

  • Sudden, visible impact: Adjusters look for evidence of a specific event. Hail marks, broken branches, or torn shingles indicate a storm caused damage.
  • Roof age and condition: Older roofs with pre-existing wear may have lower claim payouts or higher deductibles. Adjusters examine granule loss, curled shingles, and moss growth.
  • Maintenance history: If you neglected gutter cleaning or failed to trim overhanging branches, the adjuster might claim maintenance could have prevented damage. This reduces payouts.
  • Multiple damage points: Storm damage is usually widespread. Damage limited to one small area raises suspicion. Adjusters expect to see consistent damage patterns across the roof.
  • Interior damage: Water stains on ceilings or walls strengthen your claim by showing the damage is recent and affecting the home's livability.

Deductibles vary by policy and typically range from $500 to $2,500. Check your homeowners insurance documents. Storm deductibles are usually a flat amount, but some policies in Draper 84020 have percentage-based deductibles (2-5% of your home's value for wind/hail). Common Insurance Claim Mistakes to Avoid

Homeowners in Draper often make mistakes that delay claims or reduce payouts. Here's what to avoid:

  • Waiting too long to file: File within 30 days. Most policies require prompt notice, and delays suggest you weren't concerned about the damage.
  • Starting repairs before approval: If you repair damage without adjuster approval, you may not get reimbursed. Get written approval first, then proceed.
  • Relying solely on the insurance adjuster: Adjusters work for the insurance company, not you. Always get your own professional inspection to verify their findings.
  • Poor documentation: Photos taken in bad light or with poor angles won't impress adjusters. Get professional photos that clearly show damage location, scope, and severity.
  • Underestimating repair costs: Don't accept a lowball estimate without question. Frame Roofing Utah's detailed estimates often identify additional damage and increase claim payouts.
  • Ignoring water damage: Document any water stains, mold, or interior damage. This strengthens your claim and increases the claim payout amount.

Insurance Coverage for Different Types of Storm Damage

Not all storm damage is covered equally. Here's what's typically covered under homeowners insurance in Draper:

Covered damage: Hail, high winds, falling trees, and lightning strikes are almost always covered. Most policies include roof repairs up to the roof's actual cash value or replacement cost value, depending on your coverage.

Partially covered damage: Tree removal is sometimes covered only if the tree fell on your house. Damage from unmaintained branches (pre-existing hazards) may be denied or partially denied.

Not covered: Damage from regular wear, lack of maintenance, or normal weather is not covered. Flood damage requires a separate flood insurance policy.

Review your policy to understand your coverage limits. If you have questions about what's covered, Frame Roofing Utah can explain coverage as we document damage during inspections in the Draper 84020 area.
