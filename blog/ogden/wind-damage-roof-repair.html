Wind Damage Roof Repair in Ogden — What Weber County Homeowners Need to Know

April 10, 2026 · 6 min read

Ogden sits at the mouth of Ogden Canyon, one of the most significant wind corridors along Utah's Wasatch Front. When canyon winds accelerate through the narrow gap between Ben Lomond Peak and Mount Ogden, they can reach sustained speeds of 50 to 80 miles per hour with gusts exceeding 100 mph during severe events. These wind events are not rare. They happen multiple times every winter and spring, and they do serious damage to roofs throughout Weber County.

Why Ogden Roofs Take More Wind Damage Than Most

The geography of the Ogden area creates a natural wind tunnel. Cold air pooling in the mountain valleys gets pushed through canyon gaps by pressure differentials, and by the time that air reaches neighborhoods in North Ogden, Pleasant View, South Ogden, and Riverdale, it is moving fast enough to tear shingles off roofs. Homes on hillsides and ridgelines are especially vulnerable because they lack the wind protection that trees and neighboring structures provide in valley-floor neighborhoods.

Architectural shingle warranties typically cover wind speeds up to 110 or 130 mph, but that coverage assumes the shingles were installed correctly with the right number of nails in the right pattern. Older homes in Ogden's established neighborhoods near Washington Boulevard and Harrison Boulevard often have roofs that were installed with three-tab shingles and fewer fasteners than current building codes require. These roofs are the first to show wind damage.

Signs of Wind Damage on Your Roof

Wind damage is not always obvious from the ground. While a major windstorm might rip entire sections of shingles off your roof, more commonly the damage is subtle. Here is what to look for after a significant wind event in the Ogden area.

Missing shingles are the most obvious sign. If you see bare patches of underlayment or decking exposed on your roof, you need immediate attention to prevent water infiltration. Lifted or creased shingles are harder to spot but equally problematic. Wind gets under the leading edge of a shingle and bends it upward, breaking the seal strip that holds it flat. Once that seal is broken, the shingle will never lay flat again and becomes vulnerable to the next wind event.

Damaged ridge caps along the peak of your roof are a common wind casualty because ridge caps sit at the highest point and take the full force of the wind. Look for ridge cap shingles that are cracked, shifted, or missing entirely. Also check your gutters and downspouts for dents, separation from the fascia, or sections that have pulled away from the roofline.

Inside your home, look for water stains on ceilings or walls after a wind event, especially if rain accompanied the wind. Even small amounts of moisture finding their way past damaged shingles can cause significant problems over time.

Emergency Tarping After Wind Damage

If wind has exposed your roof decking or underlayment, temporary tarping is essential to prevent water damage until permanent repairs can be made. A proper emergency tarp should extend at least four feet past the damaged area on all sides and be secured with two-by-four lumber screwed through the tarp into the decking. Do not rely on sandbags or loose weights — Ogden's winds will remove them within hours.

Filing an Insurance Claim for Wind Damage

Wind damage to your roof is covered under the dwelling coverage portion of most standard homeowners insurance policies in Utah. The process starts with filing a claim through your insurance company, which will assign an adjuster to inspect the damage. Having a roofing contractor present during the adjuster's visit is one of the most important things you can do to ensure your claim is fully and fairly assessed.

Insurance adjusters inspect dozens of properties after major wind events and may spend as little as 15 to 20 minutes on your roof. A contractor who knows exactly where the damage is can guide the adjuster to every affected area and ensure the scope of work reflects the actual repair needed. At Frame Roofing Utah, we attend every adjuster meeting at no charge and will supplement the claim if the initial assessment underestimates the damage.

Utah law gives you one year from the date of the wind event to file an insurance claim. However, filing sooner is always better. Damage that goes unrepaired for months can be classified as maintenance neglect rather than storm damage, which gives your insurance company grounds to deny the claim.

Repair vs. Full Replacement After Wind Damage

Whether you need repairs or a full replacement depends on the extent of the damage and the age of your existing roof. If wind damage is isolated to one slope or a small section, targeted repairs may be sufficient. Your contractor will match the existing shingle style and color as closely as possible, replace the damaged sections, and ensure the surrounding area is properly sealed.

If wind damage is widespread, if your roof is more than 15 years old, or if this is not the first time wind has caused problems, a full replacement is often the better investment. Modern architectural shingles with enhanced wind ratings and six-nail installation patterns are significantly more resistant to Ogden's canyon winds than what was standard even ten years ago.

Protecting Your Ogden Home From Future Wind Damage

Proper installation is the single most important factor in wind resistance. Shingles installed with six nails instead of four and with starter strips along eaves and rakes can withstand dramatically higher wind speeds. High-wind hip and ridge caps provide additional protection at the most vulnerable points on your roof. Ensuring your attic has proper ventilation also helps by equalizing pressure above and below the roof deck during wind events, reducing the lifting force on your shingles.
