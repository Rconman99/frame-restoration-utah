WHY BOUNTIFUL ROOFS GET HAIL DAMAGE SO OFTEN

\n\n

Bountiful's location north of Salt Lake City puts it directly in the path of spring hailstorms that develop over the Great Salt Lake. These storms regularly dump golf-ball-sized hail across the 84010 ZIP code, and your roof takes the hit.

\n\n

Hail impact weakens the granules on asphalt shingles, creates splits in wood shakes, and can punch holes through older roofing materials. Most homeowners don't realize the damage until months later when leaks appear in the attic or ceiling.

\n\n

That's where your homeowner's insurance comes in. Hail is considered a covered peril under most policies, which means your insurance should pay for repairs. The key is filing the claim quickly and documenting everything.

DOCUMENT THE DAMAGE IMMEDIATELY

\n\n

The first step after a hail storm in Bountiful is documenting the damage before the weather clears. Insurance adjusters take photos seriously\u2014they're your evidence.

\n\n

Here's what to do right after a storm hits your Bountiful home:

\n\n
    \n
  • Take wide shots of your roof from the ground using your phone or camera. Include your house address in at least one photo (address numbers on the mailbox or garage).
    • \n
  • Photograph hail damage up close. If you can safely access your roof or use binoculars, capture dents, cracks, or missing pieces on shingles. Get at least 8-10 close-up photos showing clear impact marks.
    • \n
  • Document other damage. Photograph hail damage to gutters, downspouts, skylights, siding, or HVAC units. Insurance covers these too.
    • \n
  • Take video. Walk around your property and record a 1-2 minute video narrating the damage. This helps insurance adjusters see the full scope.
    • \n
  • Note the date and time. Write down when the storm occurred and when you took photos. Insurance timestamps matter.
    • \n
\n\n

Don't touch or repair anything yet. Adjusters need to see the damage in its original condition. Roofing work in Bountiful 84010 can wait a few days while you handle the claim process.

HOW TO TELL IF HAIL DAMAGED YOUR ROOF

\n\n

Hail damage doesn't always announce itself with a loud crash. Sometimes homeowners in Draper go weeks without realizing their roof was hit. Learning to spot the signs of hail damage is the first step to protecting your home.

\n\n

Visual Signs of Hail Impact

\n\n

The most common indicator is circular dents or bruises on shingles. Unlike straight-line damage from wind or trees, hail creates a uniform, pattern-like damage across the roof surface. In Draper 84020, homeowners often notice these dents are concentrated in one direction, depending on wind direction during the storm.

\n
    \n
  • Soft spots when you touch the shingles (indicating internal cracking)
    • \n
  • Shingles missing or peeling away at the edges
    • \n
  • Black streaks or exposed underlayment where shingles have been damaged
    • \n
  • Granules (small pebbles) missing from shingle surfaces or collected in gutters

If you see hail damage on gutters and vents, the roof almost certainly took a hit too.

WHAT COUNTS AS EMERGENCY ROOF DAMAGE IN LAYTON 84041?

\n

Not every roof issue requires emergency tarping, but certain damage clearly does. Understanding the difference helps you act fast when it matters.

\n

Signs Your Roof Needs Immediate Attention

\n

Large holes or missing sections: If you can see daylight through your roof or there's a visible gap, water is pouring into your attic right now. This is an emergency.

\n

A fallen or leaning tree: Even if the tree hasn't broken through completely, it's putting dangerous weight on your roof structure. Removal and tarping are urgent priorities.

\n

Sagging roof deck: If the roof is visibly sagging or drooping, the structural integrity is compromised. This is a safety hazard and requires immediate professional assessment.

\n

Massive shingle loss: A storm that strips off dozens or hundreds of shingles leaves your roof deck exposed to rain and UV. In Layton's dry climate, exposure time matters less than in wetter regions, but damage accelerates quickly.

\n

Flashing failures: If flashing around chimneys, vents, or skylights is torn or separated, water will find its way inside during the next rain.

What should I do immediately after a tree falls on my Layton roof?

\n
