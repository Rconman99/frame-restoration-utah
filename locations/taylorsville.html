Premium Roof Replacements in Taylorsville

Taylorsville is one of Salt Lake County's largest cities with 60,000 residents — and its established neighborhoods represent one of the biggest reroofing markets in the valley. The bulk of Taylorsville's housing stock was built during the 1970s through 1990s along the 4700 South and 5400 South corridors, near Valley Fair Mall, and throughout the residential blocks between Redwood Road and 2700 West. These homes are now 30 to 40 years old, an age where even well-maintained shingles begin failing from decades of Utah freeze-thaw cycling, summer UV exposure, and accumulated storm damage.

Taylorsville's proximity to the Jordan River adds a moisture factor that central valley communities further from the river do not face. Higher ambient humidity near the river corridor promotes algae and moss growth on north-facing roof slopes, accelerates deterioration of aging shingle adhesive, and creates conditions where ice dams form more readily in winter. Frame Roofing Utah accounts for these Jordan River corridor conditions in every Taylorsville installation — using algae-resistant shingles, enhanced ventilation, and moisture barrier systems that address the specific challenges of this location. Every project includes our 10-year workmanship warranty and lifetime manufacturer warranties.

Jordan River Moisture and Aging Shingles: Taylorsville's Roofing Reality

Taylorsville's combination of aging housing stock and Jordan River corridor moisture creates roofing conditions that require specific solutions. Frame Roofing Utah addresses every factor:
