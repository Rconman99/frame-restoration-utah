Premium Roof Replacements in Midvale

Midvale occupies a central position in the Salt Lake Valley along the I-15 corridor — a crossroads community where older single-family homes from the 1950s through 1970s sit alongside newer townhome and condo developments. The neighborhoods near Fort Union Boulevard and the 7200 South commercial corridor contain some of the most diverse housing stock in the county, from traditional ranch homes with aging three-tab shingles to modern mixed-use buildings with flat-roof membrane systems. Each requires a different roofing approach, and Frame Roofing Utah has the expertise to handle them all.

Midvale's compact lots and proximity to the Jordan River trail corridor mean that storm runoff and moisture management are critical considerations during any reroofing project. Many older Midvale homes also have inadequate attic ventilation — a common issue in 1950s-1970s construction that accelerates shingle aging and creates ice dam problems in winter. Frame Roofing Utah addresses these underlying issues during every reroof, not just the shingles on top.
