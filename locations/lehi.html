Roofing FAQs for Lehi Residents

How often does Lehi get hail damage that requires roof repair? +
Lehi's position at the north end of Utah Valley catches storms from the Point of the Mountain corridor. The city's explosive growth — from rural community to one of Utah's fastest-growing cities — means a massive mix of housing ages exposed to hail events from May through September. Frame Roofing Utah recommends a free professional inspection after any significant storm event — hail damage invisible from the ground can shorten your roof's life by 5-10 years if left unaddressed.
Do I need a building permit to replace my roof in Lehi? +
Lehi City requires building permits for roof replacements. The city's rapid growth means the Building Department is busy — Frame Roofing Utah expedites permit processing for our clients.
Does my Lehi HOA need to approve a roof replacement? +
Lehi's newer developments (Traverse Mountain, Thanksgiving Point area, North Shore) have active HOAs with architectural review requirements. Older Lehi neighborhoods along Main Street generally have no HOA restrictions.
How do I file a roof insurance claim in Lehi after a storm? +
Lehi's diverse housing ages mean insurance claims vary widely. Newer Traverse Mountain homes may have first-time claims, while older homes near Main Street face depreciation challenges. Frame Roofing Utah tailors documentation to each situation.
What roofing materials are best for Lehi's climate? +
Lehi's wind exposure at the Point of the Mountain makes high-wind-rated shingles essential. Class 4 impact resistance handles the frequent hail. Traverse Mountain homes benefit from enhanced underlayment for heavier snow loads.
How much does a roof replacement cost in Lehi? +
Roof replacement costs in Lehi typically range from $8,000 to $24,000 for a standard residential home, depending on square footage, roof pitch, material choice, and damage extent. If your roof was damaged by hail or wind, insurance may cover most or all of the replacement cost. Frame Roofing Utah provides free written estimates with no obligation.
How long does a roof replacement take in Lehi? +
Lehi reroofs take 1-3 days. HOA approval in newer developments adds 1-2 weeks. Insurance projects average 2-3 weeks.
What are the signs my Lehi roof needs replacement? +
Lehi homeowners should check for wind damage (especially near the Point of the Mountain), hail dimpling after storms, and premature aging on builder-grade shingles installed during the 2005-2015 building boom.
