Common Commercial Roof Problems in Salt Lake City

  • Flat roof ponding on downtown buildings — Many downtown SLC commercial buildings have flat roofs with inadequate drainage. Standing water accelerates membrane breakdown and causes leaks, especially after spring snowmelt.
  • HVAC unit flashing failures — Rooftop HVAC units are common on SLC commercial buildings. The flashing around these units fails over time due to thermal expansion and vibration, creating leak points.
  • Snow load on warehouse roofs — Large warehouse and industrial roofs near the I-15 corridor accumulate significant snow loads. Structural stress and ice dam formation at parapet walls are common concerns.
  • Aging TPO on 2000s-era strip malls — Many strip malls and retail centers built in the early 2000s have TPO membranes nearing the end of their lifespan. Seam failures and membrane shrinkage are increasingly common.
  • UV degradation at high altitude — Salt Lake City's elevation means stronger UV exposure year-round, which accelerates breakdown of single-ply membranes and asphalt-based systems faster than manufacturers' standard projections.

How often should flat roofs be maintained in Salt Lake City?

We recommend inspecting flat commercial roofs at least twice per year — once in spring after snow season and again in fall before winter. SLC's freeze-thaw cycles, UV exposure, and hail events can accelerate wear on TPO, EPDM, and modified bitumen systems.
