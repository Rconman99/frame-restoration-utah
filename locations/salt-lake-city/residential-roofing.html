Common Roof Problems in Salt Lake City

  • Historic homes with aging roofing — Many Avenues and Capitol Hill homes still have original slate or wood shake roofs that need modern upgrades to meet current building codes and withstand Utah weather.
  • Lake-effect snow and ice dams — Older Avenues homes with inadequate attic insulation are especially prone to ice dams from lake-effect snowfall, which backs water under shingles and causes interior damage.
  • UV degradation from high altitude — At 4,200+ feet, Salt Lake City's UV exposure is significantly higher than sea level. Asphalt shingles break down faster than homeowners expect, especially on south- and west-facing slopes.
  • Hail damage from Wasatch storms — SLC averages 3 to 5 significant hail events per year. Granule loss weakens shingles and leads to leaks within 12 to 24 months if not addressed.
  • Inadequate attic ventilation — Many pre-1980s Salt Lake City homes lack proper ridge and soffit ventilation, trapping heat and moisture that shortens roof life and promotes mold growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a roof replacement cost in Salt Lake City?

Most Salt Lake City roof replacements range from $8,500 to $20,000 depending on roof size, pitch, material, and whether tear-off is needed. Historic homes in the Avenues or Federal Heights may cost more due to steeper pitches and access challenges. We provide a free, itemized estimate so there are no surprises.

How long does a roof replacement take in Salt Lake City?

A typical Salt Lake City residential roof replacement takes one to two days. Larger homes or historic properties with complex rooflines may take up to three days. We schedule around weather and keep you informed throughout the project.

What sets Frame Restoration apart from other Salt Lake City roofers?

Uncompromising standards and a meticulous finish on every project. We use premium materials, manufacturer-trained crews, and pay special attention to preserving the architectural character of Salt Lake City's diverse home styles — from Avenues Victorians to East Bench contemporaries.

What are the best shingle types for Utah weather?

We recommend Class 4 impact-resistant architectural shingles from GAF or Owens Corning for most Salt Lake City homes. They handle hail, UV, and freeze-thaw better than standard shingles and carry enhanced manufacturer warranties. Standing-seam metal is also excellent for homes that take heavy snow loads.

Do you work with HOAs in Salt Lake City?

Yes. We regularly work with HOAs across Salt Lake City and understand the approval processes, color restrictions, and material requirements. We can provide spec sheets and color samples to submit to your HOA board before work begins.
