Common Storm Damage in Salt Lake City

  • Hail damage from Wasatch supercells — SLC averages 3 to 5 significant hail events per year. Hailstones bruise shingles, crack tiles, and dent metal roofing and flashing — often causing damage that isn't visible from the ground.
  • Wind damage from canyon winds — Powerful wind gusts funnel through Parley's Canyon and Emigration Canyon, lifting shingles, tearing off ridge caps, and breaking tree limbs that fall on roofs across the east side of the city.
  • Ice dams on Avenues and East Bench homes — Older homes in the Avenues, Federal Heights, and East Bench frequently develop ice dams due to inadequate attic ventilation. Backed-up water seeps under shingles and causes interior ceiling and wall damage.
  • Heavy snow loads on flat-roofed buildings — Downtown commercial buildings and mid-century homes with flat or low-slope roofs accumulate dangerous snow loads. Structural stress and meltwater intrusion are common after heavy snowfall events.
  • Wind-driven rain penetration — During intense Wasatch thunderstorms, wind-driven rain forces water behind flashing, into improperly sealed valleys, and around chimney and skylight penetrations — even on relatively new roofs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does insurance cover hail damage to my Salt Lake City roof?

In most cases, yes. Homeowner's insurance typically covers hail damage to roofing materials. We meet with your adjuster on-site, document every detail with photos and measurements, and handle all supplemental paperwork to maximize your claim — at no extra cost to you.

How quickly can you respond to storm damage in Salt Lake City?

We offer 24/7 emergency response for storm damage in the Salt Lake City area. In most cases, we can have a crew on-site within a few hours to tarp exposed areas, prevent further water intrusion, and begin documenting the damage for your insurance claim.

How can I spot hail damage on my roof?

Common signs of hail damage include dented or bruised shingles, granule loss (check your gutters and downspouts), cracked or missing shingles, dented metal flashing or vents, and dings on gutters or downspouts. If you suspect damage after a storm, call us for a free inspection — hail damage isn't always visible from the ground.

What if my insurance claim is denied?

If your claim is initially denied, we can help you file a supplement with additional documentation, photos, and measurements. We've successfully overturned many denied claims by providing thorough evidence of storm damage that adjusters may have missed during their initial inspection.

Do you provide emergency tarping after storm damage?

Yes. Emergency tarping is one of our most common storm response services. We secure exposed areas quickly to prevent further water damage to your home's interior. The cost of emergency tarping is typically covered by your homeowner's insurance as part of the storm damage claim.
