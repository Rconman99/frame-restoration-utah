Why Salt Lake City Homeowners Call Frame Restoration for Repairs

Salt Lake City's weather puts roofs through a punishing cycle: heavy snow in winter, rapid freeze-thaw from November through March, intense UV in summer at 4,200+ feet, and hail from Wasatch thunderstorms. That combination causes flashing to fail, shingles to crack, and small leaks to develop — often without homeowners noticing until water stains appear on ceilings.

Frame Restoration specializes in targeted roof repairs that fix the actual problem — not just the visible symptom. We trace every leak to its source, whether it's failed chimney flashing on an older Avenues home, a cracked valley on a multi-gable Sugar House bungalow, or a blown-off ridge cap on an East Bench property hit by canyon winds.

We're honest about when a repair makes sense and when a replacement is the better investment. If your roof is under 15 years old and the damage is localized, a repair can extend its life by years. If the damage is widespread or the roof is past its lifespan, we'll tell you that too — no pressure, just straight answers.

Common Roof Repair Issues in Salt Lake City

  • Flashing failures around chimneys — Older Salt Lake City homes, especially in the Avenues and Capitol Hill, commonly develop leaks where chimney flashing has cracked, separated, or corroded after years of freeze-thaw cycling.
  • Cracked and missing shingles from freeze-thaw — SLC's daily temperature swings in winter cause shingles to expand and contract repeatedly. Over time, this leads to cracking, curling, and shingles breaking free — leaving the underlayment exposed.
  • Valley leak repairs on complex rooflines — Many Salt Lake City homes have multi-gable or cross-hip rooflines with valleys that collect water and debris. Improperly sealed valleys are one of the most common leak sources we repair.
  • Rubber boot failures around plumbing vents — The rubber boots (pipe jacks) around plumbing vent pipes dry out and crack in Utah's UV and temperature extremes. A failed boot is a guaranteed leak point that's easy and affordable to fix.
  • Gutter and soffit damage from ice backup — Ice buildup along eaves pushes behind gutters and into soffit boards, causing rot and water intrusion into the attic and exterior walls. We repair the damage and address the ventilation issue causing it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a roof repair cost in Salt Lake City?

Minor roof repairs in Salt Lake City typically range from $300 to $1,500 depending on the type and extent of damage. Flashing repairs, small leak patches, and shingle replacements fall on the lower end, while valley repairs or larger sections cost more. We provide a free estimate before any work begins.

Should I repair or replace my Salt Lake City roof?

If damage is limited to a small area and your roof is under 15 years old, a repair is usually the best option. If your roof is 20+ years old, has widespread damage, or has had multiple repairs, a full replacement is often more cost-effective in the long run. We'll give you an honest recommendation during our free inspection.

Do you offer emergency roof repairs in Salt Lake City?

Yes. We offer 24/7 emergency roof repair response across Salt Lake City. Whether it's an active leak during a rainstorm or wind damage that's left your roof exposed, we can typically have a crew on-site within a few hours to secure the area and prevent further damage.

Can small roof leaks be patched instead of replacing the whole roof?

In many cases, yes. Small leaks caused by cracked flashing, a few missing shingles, or a failed pipe boot can be repaired without replacing the entire roof. We identify the exact source of the leak and fix it properly — not just the visible symptom. We'll always be upfront if a repair won't hold and a replacement is the better investment.
