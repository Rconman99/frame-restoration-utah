Why Sandy Homeowners Choose Frame Restoration for Solar Installation

Most solar installers are electricians who bolt panels onto your roof. We're roofers first. That means we understand how penetrations affect your roof's waterproofing, how panel placement impacts drainage, and how to install solar in a way that actually protects your roof instead of compromising it.

Sandy's south-facing slopes, high elevation, and 225+ annual sunny days make it one of the best cities in Utah for residential solar. Homes in neighborhoods like Pepperwood, White City, and along the east bench get excellent sun exposure with minimal shade interference.

Common Solar Installation Problems in Sandy

  • Solar installed on an aging roof — Too many Sandy homeowners get solar installed on a 15-year-old roof, then need a costly panel removal and reinstall when the roof fails a few years later. We assess and address roof condition first.
  • Poor flashing around panel mounts — Every solar panel attachment is a roof penetration. Improperly flashed mounts lead to leaks that can go unnoticed for months under the panel array.
  • Shade from Wasatch terrain — Some Sandy homes near the canyon mouths lose afternoon sun to mountain shadows. Proper site analysis ensures your system is positioned for maximum production.
  • HOA pushback on panel placement — Some Sandy HOAs have aesthetic guidelines for solar. While Utah law protects your right to solar, we design systems and handle approvals to keep the process smooth.
  • Undersized systems — Cookie-cutter solar proposals don't account for Sandy's high-altitude UV intensity or seasonal variation. We design systems based on your actual energy usage and local production data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Solar Installation FAQ — Sandy, UT

How much does solar installation cost in Sandy?

A typical residential solar system in Sandy costs between $15,000 and $30,000 before incentives. The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) currently covers 30% of the installed cost, and Utah offers additional incentives. Most Sandy homeowners see payback in 6 to 9 years with significantly lower energy bills from day one.

Should I replace my roof before installing solar?

If your roof is more than 15 years old, we strongly recommend replacing it before solar goes on. Removing and reinstalling panels for a future roof replacement adds $3,000 to $5,000 to the project. We handle both as one coordinated project to save you time and money.

Will solar work on my Sandy home if I face north?

South-facing roofs produce the most energy, but east and west-facing slopes still generate significant power — typically 75% to 85% of a south-facing system. True north-facing roofs are generally not good candidates. We'll evaluate your specific layout during the free assessment.

Does solar affect my roof warranty?

When installed properly by a qualified roofer, solar should not void your roof warranty. Because we're roofers first, we install solar with the same flashing and waterproofing standards we use on every roofing project. We stand behind both the roof and the solar installation.
