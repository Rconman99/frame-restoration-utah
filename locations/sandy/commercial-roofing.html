Common Commercial Roofing Problems in Sandy

  • Ponding water on flat roofs — Sandy's freeze-thaw cycles create and worsen low spots where water pools. Ponding water is the leading cause of premature membrane failure on commercial buildings.
  • Seam separation on TPO and EPDM — Temperature swings from summer highs above 100°F to winter lows below 0°F stress membrane seams. Poorly welded seams open up and leak.
  • Snow load on large-span buildings — Sandy warehouses and retail buildings with wide roof spans are particularly vulnerable to heavy snow loads. Inadequate structural support leads to sagging and eventual failure.
  • Clogged commercial drains — Gravel, debris, and mineral buildup clog internal drains and scuppers. Backed-up drainage compounds ponding problems and adds thousands of pounds of weight.
  • HVAC and rooftop penetration leaks — Commercial roofs in Sandy are crowded with HVAC units, vents, and conduit penetrations. Each one is a potential leak point if not properly flashed and sealed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Commercial Roofing FAQ — Sandy, UT

What commercial roofing systems do you install in Sandy?

We install TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, built-up roofing (BUR), and standing-seam metal systems. The best choice depends on your building's size, slope, budget, and energy goals. TPO is our most popular system for Sandy commercial properties because of its reflectivity and durability.

How long does a commercial roof last in Sandy?

A properly installed TPO or EPDM system in Sandy typically lasts 20 to 30 years with regular maintenance. Metal roofs can last 40+ years. The key is quality installation and a proactive maintenance plan that addresses Sandy's specific weather challenges.

Can you work around our business hours?

Yes. We routinely schedule commercial projects around operating hours, tenant access, and noise restrictions. Many Sandy commercial re-roofs are completed in sections to keep the building operational throughout the project.

Do you offer maintenance plans for commercial roofs in Sandy?

We do. Our commercial maintenance program includes biannual inspections, drain clearing, seam checks, and minor repairs. Proactive maintenance catches small problems before they become expensive emergencies — especially important in Sandy's harsh climate.
