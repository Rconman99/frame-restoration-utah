Common General Contracting Problems in Sandy

  • Outdated kitchens in 1990s-era homes — Many Sandy neighborhoods were built 25 to 30 years ago. Original kitchens with laminate countertops, builder-grade cabinets, and dated layouts are ready for modern upgrades.
  • Unfinished basements — Sandy homes commonly have 800 to 1,200 square feet of unfinished basement space. Finishing this space is one of the highest-ROI improvements a Sandy homeowner can make.
  • Growing families needing additions — Sandy's family-friendly neighborhoods attract growing families who need extra bedrooms, expanded living areas, or accessory dwelling units.
  • Bathroom deterioration — Tile grout, caulking, and plumbing fixtures from the original build start failing after 20+ years, leading to water damage behind walls and under floors.
  • HOA and permit complexity — Sandy's HOA landscape can be tricky for home additions and exterior remodels. We handle the approval process so you don't have to navigate it alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

General Contracting FAQ — Sandy, UT

What types of general contracting work do you do in Sandy?

We handle kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, basement finishes, home additions, structural framing, flooring, drywall, and paint. If it involves building or remodeling inside or outside your Sandy home, we likely do it. The only work we don't handle is standalone electrical or plumbing — though we coordinate those trades as part of larger projects.

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Sandy?

Sandy kitchen remodels typically range from $25,000 to $75,000 depending on scope. A cosmetic refresh (countertops, cabinets, fixtures) runs $25,000 to $40,000. A full gut renovation with layout changes, new plumbing, and electrical runs $50,000 to $75,000+. We provide a detailed estimate before any work begins.

Do you handle permits for Sandy projects?

Yes. We pull all required building permits from Sandy City and manage the inspection process throughout construction. For projects in HOA communities, we also handle the architectural review and approval process.

How long does a typical Sandy home remodel take?

A bathroom remodel takes 3 to 5 weeks. A kitchen remodel takes 6 to 10 weeks. A home addition takes 10 to 16 weeks depending on size and complexity. We provide a detailed schedule at the start of every project and keep you updated on progress.

Are you licensed as a general contractor in Utah?

Yes. Frame Restoration holds a Utah general contractor license and carries full general liability and workers' compensation insurance. We're licensed to handle residential construction, renovation, and restoration projects across the state.
