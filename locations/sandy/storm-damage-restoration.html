Common Storm Damage Restoration Problems in Sandy

  • Hail damage that's hard to see from the ground — Hail can bruise shingles without breaking them. The granule loss is invisible from street level but accelerates aging and leads to leaks within a year.
  • Wind-lifted shingles and exposed nail heads — Canyon-driven gusts peel back shingle edges and expose fasteners. Once a nail head is exposed, water wicks under the shingle and into your roof deck.
  • Heavy snow collapse on older roofs — Sandy's annual snowfall averages 55+ inches. Older roofs with weakened trusses or decking are at risk of sagging or partial collapse during heavy snow events.
  • Ice dam water intrusion — Rapid freeze-thaw cycles along the east bench create ice dams that push water under shingles and into attic spaces, causing ceiling stains and mold.
  • Delayed damage claims — Many Sandy homeowners don't realize they have storm damage until months later when leaks appear. Insurance companies have filing deadlines, so prompt inspections are critical.

Frequently Asked Questions

Storm Damage Restoration FAQ — Sandy, UT

How do I know if my Sandy roof has storm damage?

Look for missing or cracked shingles, dented gutters, granules in your downspouts, and ceiling stains inside your home. However, many types of hail damage are invisible from the ground. We provide free roof inspections after storms and can identify damage that most homeowners miss.

Will insurance cover my Sandy storm damage repair?

In most cases, yes. If hail, wind, or a fallen tree caused the damage, your homeowner's policy typically covers the repair minus your deductible. We handle the entire claims process and work directly with your adjuster to maximize your coverage.

How quickly can you respond after a storm in Sandy?

We respond within 24 hours for storm damage assessments in Sandy. If your roof is actively leaking, we provide same-day emergency tarping to prevent further interior damage while your claim is processed.

Do I have to pay anything out of pocket for storm damage?

You're responsible for your insurance deductible — that's it. We work directly with your insurance company so there are no surprise costs. We never inflate claims or charge beyond what the insurance company approves.

How long do I have to file a storm damage claim in Sandy?

Most Utah homeowner's policies require you to file within one year of the storm event. However, we strongly recommend getting your roof inspected as soon as possible. The longer you wait, the harder it becomes to prove the damage was storm-related.
