Common Storm Damage Restoration Problems in Sandy

  • Hail damage that's hard to see from the ground — Hail can bruise shingles without breaking them. The granule loss is invisible from street level but accelerates aging and leads to leaks within a year.
  • Wind-lifted shingles and exposed nail heads — Canyon-driven gusts peel back shingle edges and expose fasteners. Once a nail head is exposed, water wicks under the shingle and into your roof deck.
  • Heavy snow collapse on older roofs — Sandy's annual snowfall averages 55+ inches. Older roofs with weakened trusses or decking are at risk of sagging or partial collapse during heavy snow events.
  • Ice dam water intrusion — Rapid freeze-thaw cycles along the east bench create ice dams that push water under shingles and into attic spaces, causing ceiling stains and mold.
  • Delayed damage discovery — Many Sandy homeowners don't realize they have storm damage until months later when leaks appear. The longer damage goes unaddressed, the more extensive and costly repairs become. Prompt professional inspections are critical.
