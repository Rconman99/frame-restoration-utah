Common Water, Fire & Flood Restoration Problems in Sandy

  • Roof leak water damage — Storm-driven leaks are the most common source of water damage in Sandy homes. Water travels down rafters and walls, often causing damage far from the original leak point.
  • Frozen pipe bursts — Sandy's winter lows drop well below freezing. Pipes in exterior walls, crawl spaces, and uninsulated areas burst during cold snaps, flooding finished basements and main floors.
  • Spring snowmelt flooding — Rapid snowmelt in April and May pushes groundwater levels up. Sandy homes near canyon drainages and Dimple Dell are particularly vulnerable to basement water intrusion.
  • Kitchen and bathroom fires — Cooking fires, electrical shorts, and bathroom exhaust issues cause localized fires that produce heavy smoke and soot damage throughout the home.
  • Delayed mold growth — Water that isn't fully extracted and dried within 48 to 72 hours leads to mold growth behind walls and under floors. Professional restoration with proper drying equipment prevents this.
