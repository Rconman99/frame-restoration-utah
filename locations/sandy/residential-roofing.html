Common Residential Roofing Problems in Sandy

  • Hail-bruised shingles — Sandy experiences multiple hailstorms per year. Granule loss weakens shingles and leads to leaks within 12 to 24 months if not addressed.
  • Ice dams along eaves — Homes near the canyon mouths see heavy snow accumulation. Poor attic ventilation causes ice dams that back water under shingles.
  • Canyon wind uplift — East-bench homes in ZIP 84094 get hit with 60+ mph gusts funneling out of the canyons. Improperly sealed shingles lift, crack, and blow off.
  • Aging architectural shingles — Many Sandy neighborhoods were built in the 1990s and early 2000s. Roofs from that era are now 25+ years old and past their effective lifespan.
  • UV degradation — Sandy averages 225+ sunny days per year. UV breaks down asphalt shingles faster than homeowners expect, especially on south-facing slopes.

Residential Roofing FAQ — Sandy, UT

How much does a residential roof replacement cost in Sandy?

Most Sandy roof replacements fall between $8,000 and $18,000 depending on size, pitch, material, and whether tear-off is required. Premium upgrades like impact-resistant shingles or standing-seam metal fall at the higher end. We provide a free, itemized estimate so there are no surprises.

How long does a roof replacement take in Sandy?

A typical Sandy residential roof replacement takes one to two days. Larger or steeper homes may take up to three days. We schedule around weather and keep you updated throughout the project.

What sets Frame Restoration apart from other Sandy roofers?

Uncompromising standards on every project. We use premium materials, manufacturer-trained crews, and a multi-point quality inspection process. Every roof we install meets or exceeds manufacturer specifications for ventilation, underlayment, and flashing.

What roofing materials do you recommend for Sandy homes?

For most Sandy homes, we recommend impact-resistant (Class 4) architectural shingles from GAF or Owens Corning. They handle hail, wind, and UV better than standard shingles and carry enhanced manufacturer warranties. Metal roofing is another strong option for east-bench homes that take heavy snow.

Are you licensed and insured for roofing in Sandy, UT?

Absolutely. Frame Restoration is fully licensed and insured in the state of Utah. We carry general liability and workers' compensation coverage on every job, and we're happy to provide proof on request.
