Mountain-Grade Reroofing for Heber City Homes

Frame Roofing Utah is headquartered in Heber City — 142 S Main St, in the heart of Wasatch County. We are not a national chain driving in from Salt Lake City. We are your neighbors.

Heber Valley sits at 5,600 feet elevation and receives more than 100 inches of snow per year — the third-highest snowfall in all of Utah. That extreme climate changes everything about how a roof needs to be built. Ice dams form along eaves when attic heat melts snowpack and it refreezes at the cold overhang. Freeze-thaw cycles crack underlayment that would survive decades in milder climates. UV radiation at elevation degrades shingles faster than at sea level. Wind channeling through Daniels Canyon and off the Wasatch Range creates uplift pressures that standard installations underestimate.

Frame Roofing Utah installs roofs built for this climate — not adapted from a template designed for Phoenix or Dallas. Every Heber City installation includes ice and water shield at all eaves and valleys, ventilation systems engineered for the freeze-thaw cycle, and materials rated for snow loads and UV exposure at elevation. We pull Wasatch County permits, coordinate with local inspectors, and know every HOA in the valley.
