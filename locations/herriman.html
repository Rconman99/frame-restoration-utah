Premium Roof Replacements in Herriman

Herriman exploded from a small town to one of Utah's largest cities in barely two decades. Almost every home in the city was built after 2005 during the rapid-growth period — Copper Mountain, Rosecrest, Herriman Towne Center, and dozens of sprawling subdivisions went up fast. Why Herriman's Builder-Grade Roofs Are Failing Early

The mass-production construction boom that built Herriman prioritized speed over durability. Frame Roofing Utah sees the same patterns across the city — and we know exactly how to fix them:

  • Builder-grade 3-tab shingles replaced with premium architectural shingles rated for 130+ mph winds and Class 4 impact resistance
  • Thin, stapled underlayment upgraded to synthetic, nail-secured underlayment that resists wind-driven rain
  • Minimum-code flashing replaced with premium metal flashing at every penetration, valley, and wall junction
  • Ventilation corrections — many Herriman homes were built with inadequate attic ventilation that accelerates shingle aging
  • Full insurance claim support for storm-damaged roofs — many Herriman homeowners qualify for full replacement through their carrier
  • Neighborhood coordination for HOAs and subdivisions where entire streets are reaching reroof age simultaneously
