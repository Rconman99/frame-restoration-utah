Roofing FAQs for Sandy Residents

Does Sandy's elevation affect roofing needs? +
Absolutely. Sandy spans from roughly 4,400 feet near I-15 to over 5,500 feet at the canyon mouths. Higher-elevation homes in the foothills face heavier snow loads, more intense hail, and greater freeze-thaw stress than valley-floor properties. We adjust materials and installation methods accordingly.
When should I replace my roof in Sandy? +
Most asphalt shingle roofs in the Salt Lake Valley last 20-30 years. If your Sandy home was built in the 1980s or 1990s — especially if it's been through multiple hailstorms — it's likely at or past its rated lifespan. We offer free inspections to honestly assess remaining life.
Does Frame Roofing Utah handle insurance claims in Sandy? +
Yes. We specialize in storm damage claims throughout Salt Lake County including Sandy. We document all hail, wind, and storm damage, then coordinate directly with your adjuster to ensure you receive the full payout your policy allows.
How much does a roof replacement cost in Sandy? +
Residential roof replacements in Sandy typically range from $9,000 to $25,000 depending on home size, roof pitch, and material selection. East bench homes with steeper pitches and greater elevation tend toward the higher end. Many homeowners pay little to nothing when storm damage is covered by insurance.
Is Frame Roofing Utah licensed in Salt Lake County? +
Yes. Frame Roofing Utah is fully licensed and insured throughout Utah including Sandy and all of Salt Lake County. We carry full general liability and workers' compensation coverage on every project.
