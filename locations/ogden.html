Premium Roof Replacements in Ogden

Ogden sits at the mouth of Ogden Canyon where storms funnel off the Wasatch Range with particular intensity. Hail events in Weber County regularly exceed golf-ball size, and the city's older housing stock — much of it built between the 1890s and 1960s — often carries roofing well past its rated lifespan. Frame Roofing Utah understands that Ogden homeowners need a roofer who knows the difference between a historic Craftsman bungalow on Madison Avenue and a split-level in Shadow Valley. We select materials and installation methods that match your home's era and architecture, not just a one-size-fits-all approach.

Weber County's freeze-thaw cycle is among the most severe along the Wasatch Front, with overnight lows dropping below zero in December through February while daytime temps climb above freezing. This daily expansion and contraction punishes roofing systems relentlessly. Frame Roofing Utah uses ice-and-water shield underlayment on all eave lines and valleys, premium synthetic felt across the full deck, and impact-resistant shingles rated for Class 4 hail — because Ogden roofs earn every bit of protection we give them.

Roofing FAQs for Ogden Residents

Do you work on historic homes in Ogden? +
Absolutely. Many of Ogden's finest homes date to the 1890s-1920s railroad era, and we've reroofed Victorians, Craftsman bungalows, and Tudor-style homes along Jefferson Avenue, 25th Street, and throughout the east bench. We select materials that complement each home's architectural period while delivering modern storm protection.
How bad is hail damage in Ogden? +
Weber County is one of Utah's most active hail zones. Storms funnel through Ogden Canyon and hit the city's east side with particular force from May through September. We recommend a free inspection after any significant storm — even if damage isn't visible from the ground, hidden granule loss can shorten your roof's lifespan by years.
Does Frame Roofing Utah handle insurance claims for Ogden homeowners? +
Yes. We specialize in storm damage claims throughout Weber County. We document all hail, wind, and storm damage with detailed photography and measurements, then coordinate directly with your adjuster to ensure you receive the full payout your policy allows.
How much does a roof replacement cost in Ogden? +
Residential roof replacements in Ogden typically range from $8,000 to $22,000 depending on home size, roof pitch, and material selection. Many Ogden homeowners pay little to nothing out of pocket when storm damage is covered by insurance. We provide free written estimates with no obligation.
Is Frame Roofing Utah licensed to work in Weber County? +
Yes. Frame Roofing Utah is fully licensed and insured throughout Utah, including Ogden and all of Weber County. We carry full general liability insurance and workers' compensation on every job.
