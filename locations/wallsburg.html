Roofing Built for Wallsburg's Mountain Climate

Wallsburg properties deal with conditions most Wasatch Front homes never see — sustained sub-zero temperatures, three to four feet of snow sitting on the roof for weeks at a time, and ice dams that form along eaves when daytime sun warms the deck while nights stay well below freezing. Frame Roofing Utah selects materials and installation methods specifically for these challenges: ice-and-water shield on all eaves and valleys, proper ridge and soffit ventilation to minimize ice damming, and fastener patterns rated for the wind exposure common in open valley settings.
