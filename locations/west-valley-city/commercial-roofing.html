Common Commercial Roofing Problems in West Valley City

  • Aging flat roofs on 3500 South commercial strip — Many retail buildings along 3500 South were built in the 1980s and 90s with built-up roofing that has reached the end of its useful life. Cracks, blisters, and seam failures are widespread.
  • Ponding on warehouse roofs in industrial corridor — Large warehouse roofs with inadequate drainage develop standing water after rain and snowmelt. Ponding accelerates membrane breakdown and leads to interior leaks.
  • TPO membrane failures on 15+ year buildings — Early TPO installations across WVC are now showing seam separation, shrinkage, and punctures. These roofs need professional re-seaming or full replacement.
  • HVAC equipment damaging membranes — Rooftop HVAC units on commercial buildings cause foot traffic wear, vibration damage, and improper flashing around penetrations — all leading to chronic leaks.
  • Deteriorating built-up roofing on older retail plazas — Multi-layer BUR systems on WVC's older retail plazas are splitting and losing gravel. Patch-over-patch repairs eventually fail, and a full replacement becomes the only solution.

If your commercial building is showing signs of roof failure — ponding, interior leaks, membrane bubbling, or visible wear — schedule a free commercial roof inspection or call us directly at 435-292-8802.

Our commercial inspection covers the full membrane surface, seams, flashing, drains, scuppers, HVAC penetrations, and parapet walls. We document everything with photos and measurements, then deliver a written report with repair or replacement recommendations and a transparent estimate.

How long does a flat commercial roof last in West Valley City?

A properly installed TPO or EPDM roof lasts 20 to 30 years in the West Valley City climate. Built-up roofing (BUR) can last 15 to 25 years. Lifespan depends on material quality, installation, maintenance, and whether ponding water is addressed. Regular inspections extend roof life significantly.
