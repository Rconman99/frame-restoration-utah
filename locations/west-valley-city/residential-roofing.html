Common Residential Roofing Problems in West Valley City

  • Aging 1970s-80s 3-tab shingles overdue for tear-off — Many Hunter and Granger homes still have original or second-layer 3-tab shingles well past their 20-year lifespan. These roofs need a full tear-off, not another layer.
  • Original wood shake in older Hunter homes — Some of West Valley City's earliest homes were built with cedar shake that has deteriorated over decades. These roofs are a fire risk and require complete replacement.
  • Hail damage on single-story ranches — WVC's large inventory of single-story ranch homes means maximum roof exposure to hailstones. These wide, low-slope roofs take the full impact of every storm.
  • Poor ventilation in original attic designs — Older WVC homes were built before modern ventilation standards. Inadequate ridge and soffit venting causes heat buildup that accelerates shingle failure and promotes ice dams.
  • Budget builder roofing failing near Mountain View Corridor — Newer subdivisions along the Mountain View Corridor often used builder-grade materials and minimal underlayment. These roofs show premature wear within 10 to 15 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Residential Roofing FAQ — West Valley City, UT

How much does a residential roof replacement cost in West Valley City?

Most West Valley City roof replacements range from $7,500 to $16,000 depending on roof size, pitch, material choice, and whether a full tear-off is needed. We provide a free, itemized estimate with no hidden fees. If your roof was damaged by hail or wind, insurance often covers most or all of the replacement cost.

Is upgrading from 3-tab to architectural shingles worth it in West Valley City?

Absolutely. Architectural shingles last 25 to 30 years compared to 15 to 20 for 3-tab, handle hail and wind far better, and improve curb appeal. For West Valley City homes built in the 1970s and 80s still running original 3-tab, the upgrade pays for itself in longevity and insurance premium reductions — especially with a Class 4 impact-resistant option.

Does insurance cover hail damage on West Valley City roofs?

In most cases, yes. West Valley City sits in the Wasatch Front hail belt, and homeowners insurance typically covers hail damage minus your deductible. We handle the entire claims process — meeting with your adjuster, documenting damage, and filing supplements — so you don't have to navigate it alone.

How long does a roof replacement take in West Valley City?

A typical West Valley City residential roof replacement takes one to two days. Single-story ranch-style homes, which are common in WVC, are often completed in a single day. We schedule around weather and keep you informed throughout the project.

Do I need a permit for a roof replacement in West Valley City?

Yes. West Valley City requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Frame Restoration handles the permit application and inspection scheduling as part of every project — it's included in our process, so you don't have to deal with city hall.
